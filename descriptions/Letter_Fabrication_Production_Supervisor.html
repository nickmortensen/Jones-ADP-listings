Job Description:

Our De Pere, WI facility is looking for a Letter Fabrication Production Supervisor to join our team.

The Production Supervisor is a key member of our growing company and will be responsible for coordinating production processes through supervision of assigned employees and areas of responsibility such as assembly, routing, trim, fabrication, digital print and vinyl.  You will lead your team as you manufacture custom signs for our local, national, and major clients while ensuring safety, quality and deliverable standards are met daily.  The position is challenged with coordinating, prioritizing, delegating assignments, training and directly ensuring work orders are completed as scheduled to meet client demands within the assigned areas.

If you are driven, thrive in a dynamic environment, and want to accomplish your vision, Jones Sign is the perfect fit for you!

This position is located in De Pere WI.

Duties and Expectations:

  • Daily scheduling of various departments within your assigned area:
    1. Digital print
    2. Routing
    3. Trim
    4. Assembly
    5. Vinyl Application
  • Planning execution of jobs with operations team by directing and leading a team of hourly members
  • Comprehending work orders, shop drawings, bill of material, and narratives and clearly communicating the information to employees
  • Gaining commitments by reviewing hourly expectations of what is required to be fabricated/assembled each day
  • Manage production schedule to meet and/or exceed budget hours
  • Controlling departmental inventory for product on shop floor
  • Manage Quality Control processes to ensure production work meets project specification leading to a reduction of rework
  • Understanding and evaluating of fabrication methods for maximum efficiency
  • Interacting with Operations Manager to assure clear communication and maximum job throughput
  • Participating member of the safety team
  • Maintaining LEAN and continuous improvement processes
  • Implementing a team concept into fabrication to help problem solve and improve on efficiencies
  • Managing employees to ensure they are performing to expectations to which they have committed
  • Being visible and accessible on the shop floor throughout each day to field employee questions and monitor job progress
  • Hiring and training employees
  • Train and delegate appropriate tasks to individuals within your assigned department
  • Completing annual performance reviews and disciplinary action when needed
  • Any other work assigned by the Operations Manager
  • Work conditions consist primarily of manufacturing environment

Qualifications:

  • An educational background in Management, Business or technical field is preferred
  • 5-10 years of supervisory experience preferred
  • Ability to read and interpret mechanical, architectural and production drawings
  • Experience creating and maintaining excellent employee relations
  • Production experience in a graphics, custom workshop or manufacturing desired
  • A creative problem solver able to identify solutions to unique situations through materials utilization and reverse engineering
  • Proficient knowledge of Microsoft Office products and Google apps
  • Ability to communicate effectively in conversation and writing
  • Ability to train and lead others Excellent follow up skills
  • Work conditions consist primarily of manufacturing environment
